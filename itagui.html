CITY OF ITAGUI

History of Itagüí

Founded in 1743, Itagüí was declared a municipality in 1832. Its name comes some historians of an indigenous chief named Bitagüí. Two of its most representative Diego Echavarría Misas and Eladio Vélez. There were two chapels in Itagüí in colonial times. One in the place of the Tablaza that had been owned by Bruno Saldarriaga, built thanks to the permission that the Bishop of Popayán Francisco José de Figueredo granted December 19, 1743. Francisco Riaza and Bruno Saldarriaga who Lot more to the small temple, gave an extension of thirty blocks for the drawing of a population. This chapel was what later came to be elevated to the status of parish.

Oficial seal of Itagüí

The coat of arms of Itagüí is the heraldic emblem that represents the Colombian town of Itagüí, in the department of Antioquia. The coat of arms was designed in 1984 by Hernán Escobar Escobar, former director of the Historical Archive of Antioquia, and officially adopted by the Municipal Council through Agreement No. 002 of March 15, 1996.

Our Lady of the Rosary parish

The Church of Our Lady of the Rosary is a Colombian temple of Catholic worship, dedicated to the Virgin Mary under the advocation of the Rosary, is located on the eastern side of the Main Park of the municipality of Itagüí, Antioquia. The plant is in the form of crux conmissa or cross of San Antonio, consists of three longitudinal naves, the main one and two lateral, in turn crossed by the transept or transverse nave that forms the arms of the cross.

Simon Bolivar park

Simón Bolívar Park is a Colombian urban park located in the city of Itagüí. Like the Simón Bolívar Park in Bello, and the Berrío Park in Medellín, the park is the axis of the municipality's road nomenclature: on its northeastern side is Calle 51, on the southeastern side is Carrera 50, on the southwestern side is Calle 50, and on the northwestern side is Carrera 51. In the center of the park is a sculpture of Simón Bolívar, donated by Diego Echavarría Misas and surrounded by a fountain. There are also three busts: one of Diego Echavarría, one of Santander and another of Avelino Saldarriaga Gaviria. On its southeastern side is the Church of Our Lady of the Rosary, the main and oldest of the municipality, and across the north corner, the CAMI, where the administration of the municipality is carried out. FESTIVAL AND EVENTS

The Festival of Industry

The Festival of Industry, Trade and Culture is an annual eight-day social and cultural festival held in Itagüi, Colombia. It showcases various types of music, photography, painting, dancing and theatre. La historia se remonta al año 1989. Al tener la ciudad de Itagüí una gran tradición industrial y comercial, además de una valiosa cultura se decide crear una festividad que representara esta ideología para la ciudad.

Theatre Festival

The Itagüí City Theater Festival is a cultural event in the city of Itagüí - Colombia where plays from all over the country are presented. The Festival began in 1992 in the city of Itagüí . In 1998, for the first time, a theater group from France was presented, changing the national character of the event. In 2003 a theater group from Italy participated . In 2011 the municipal government announced a new investment in the event by summoning the most important theater groups in the country.
